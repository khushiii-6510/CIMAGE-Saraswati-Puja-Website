SARASWATI PUJA

Saraswati Puja is celebrated in the Magha month (January-February). It coincides with Vasant Panchami or Shree Panchami. People place books near the goddess' statue or picture and worship the goddess. Many choose the day as a symbolic start of learning the alphabets (Hate Khori) by children. The goddess Saraswati is often depicted as a beautiful woman dressed in pure white,often seated on a white lotus, white symbolizes light, knowledge, and truth.

Devotion and Worship

The Saraswati Puja 2024 celebration at CIMAGE begins with the invocation of the goddess Saraswati through prayers and rituals. All three branches of CIMAGE (Boring Road Branch, Vivekananda Marg Branch & Patliputra Branch) come alive with colourful decorations, adorned with flowers and rangolis, creating an atmosphere of reverence and joy. Students, dressed in traditional attire gathered at their respective branches for the worship of Maa Saraswati. They took the blessings of Maa Saraswati for knowledge and success in their academic pursuit. CIMAGE Students conducted the Saraswati Puja ceremony at all the three branches (Boring Road Branch, Vivekananda Marg Branch & Patliputra Branch) with great devotion, accompanied by Vedic hymns and bhajans resonating in the air. Students and faculty members offered flowers, fruits, and sweets to the goddess Saraswati as a symbol of their reverence and gratitude. The atmosphere is filled with spirituality and positivity, fostering a sense of unity and harmony among everyone present.

Student's Project Exhibition

On the occasion of Saraswati Puja, CIMAGE students presented the exhibition of various projects such as, apna bihar, mithila paintings, Chandrayan, Mahatma Buddha, Ram Mandir, Culture of India though Map, famous monuments, Black day pulwama attack, old Bihar and developed bihar, hand shake robot, android app, lightning robots etc. Students from various departments present their research projects, innovative ideas, and prototypes, reflecting their academic excellence and practical skills.CIMAGE Director Prof. Neeraj Agrawal and guests evaluate the projects, providing valuable feedback and recognition to the budding innovators.

Cultural Performances by Students

One of the highlights of Saraswati Puja at CIMAGE is the mesmerising performances by students, showcasing their talents in various art forms. Students from all branches came together at the Patliputra branch of CIMAGE for the Cultural Program. From classical music and dance to modern theatrical presentations, the stage becomes a platform for students to express themselves and captivate the audience with their skills. Students performed Singing and dancing performances dedicated to Maa Saraswati. The drama act was also performed by the students based on various subjects such as save girl child, women empowerment, college life etc. One of the groups of students performed a Qawwali program very nicely. This cultural extravaganza adding a touch of creativity and entertainment to the celebration.

Prize Distribution

Saraswati Puja celebrations also provide a platform to celebrate and nurture talent and creativity beyond academics. Students who have showcased exceptional talent in music, dance, drama, painting, or any other form of creative expression are honoured during the prize distribution ceremony. By recognising and encouraging creativity, educational institutions foster a holistic approach to learning that values not only academic success but also artistic and cultural endeavours.

Social Work By CIMAGE Students

Each year, students come together to organise various initiatives aimed at uplifting the underprivileged through education. From giving useful materials to distributing educational kits, CIMAGE students take proactive steps to empower those who are less fortunate. This time students gave some Almirah and carpets to Ved Vidhyalay Students in Patna, and also distributed educational kits like school bags, pens, and copies to the underprivileged kids of slum areas of Patna. The students of CIMAGE are gearing up to continue their tradition of social work and community service. They understand that true knowledge is not just about what one learns in the classroom but also about how one uses that knowledge to make a difference in the world. Through their tireless efforts and unwavering commitment to social change, they embody the spirit of Goddess Saraswati and serve as beacons of hope for a brighter, more inclusive future.

Students Learned Management Skills

What sets Saraswati Puja at CIMAGE College apart is its seamless integration of management expertise into event planning and execution. Under the guidance of faculty mentors, students are responsible for logistics, finance, marketing, and hospitality, providing them with invaluable hands-on experience in event management. From budget allocation to sponsorship negotiations, students apply management principles to ensure the smooth functioning of the event, thus honing their organizational and leadership skills in a real-world setting. CIMAGE College creates an immersive experience that leaves a lasting impression on its students, shaping them into responsible leaders equipped with both knowledge and values.
